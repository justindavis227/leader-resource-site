Mission + Mantras

Who We Are

As members of the body of Christ, we seek Great Commission and Great Commandment living (Matt. 22:37–40 · Matt. 28:18–20).

As members of this local body of Christ called Southeast, we seek to live this way through connecting people to Jesus and one another, utilizing some metaphors based on the life of Christ to keep us focused.

You, as a Team Leader over a Student Ministry team, are the culture carrier of our Mission and Mantras. You should frequently use them as a GPS to keep our ministry efforts focused on accomplishing Great Commission and Great Commandment living.

Team Leaders

The Heart of a Shepherd

You win when your team feels cared for, knows where they are going, and accomplishes the mission God has given us. Keep the heart of a shepherd at the forefront of everything you do.

The primary reason you have this manual is to help you better shepherd your team over time. In this section are key resources around expectations, building teams, developing others, and helping new leaders.

In further sections, you’ll be teed up for Key Conversations with the individuals on your team—a mix of planned and “as you go” conversations. To help unify the individuals you lead, there is also a section dedicated to facilitating Team Dialogues.

Both are there for your support. You are free to add for your team’s context, but this is the minimum we expect you to follow!

1-on-1s with Your Team

Key Conversations

Throughout the semester you’ll have plenty of side conversations, hall conversations, random, text and phone conversations with your team.

However, a shepherd doesn’t randomly walk with his sheep. A shepherd has a plan for where he is taking his sheep.

The following pages are Key Conversations to have with individuals on your team, meant to be led one-on-one or, if necessary, one-on-a-few. The reason is simple: honesty and vulnerability can be hard to achieve in group settings. Pulling teammates aside for these conversations over time will be fruitful.

Depending on the size of your team, you might need more or less time. They will require you to intentionally put days and times on the calendar. Our prayer is that these conversations grow your friendships with your teammates, as well as set you up to lead them like Jesus.

Monthly Team Meetings

Team Dialogues

Each month, pull your team together to dialogue about important details of your ministry. These meetings should be planned, preferably at a recurring time, for the best turnout. Identify with your team the best day and time for everyone.

At times these meetings will require sacrifice for some teammates. If good friendships are building, over time you’ll find a rhythm and people will naturally make it a priority to spend time together as a team.

Never cancel your standing meeting—you may do more harm than good to the culture of consistency. If someone can’t make it, relay the important details.

These monthly subjects don’t need your entire time together. We hope you share meals, hang out, and enjoy one another as part of these times!

05 · Big Three41
Preceding Each of the Big Three

Unleashed Leaders (All Year)

Unleashed Leaders is a deep dive into our “why”—a training we expect every new leader to go through.

It sets up new leaders to learn who we are as a Student Ministry. Whether someone joins to lead a group or serve at check-in, they need to know our primary aim is to make disciples. We look at the life of Christ and allow it to inform how we approach ministry.

Since Jesus himself continually raised the bar for his disciples, we inform those joining us about the cost of following Jesus up front, so later they aren’t surprised by what we ask of them. In some ways it can appear we are talking people out of doing ministry. In actuality, we are just echoing Jesus’ call to make disciples—even some who heard it straight from Jesus’ mouth chose not to follow.

We know things happen—but being up front about the commitment pays dividends when leaders know what they are signing up for and stick it out. We love the community of leaders God has blessed us with. We get to fulfill Jesus’ mission together!

Five Things to Define

Building an Effective Team

Define Goals & Purpose Up Front Teams that lack direction usually fizzle out
  • How will your team practically live on mission?
  • Can you rally around a common goal?
Define Framework Structure to operate within
  • How does your team share responsibilities?
  • Does your team understand how things are supposed to work?
Define Communication The thread that keeps everyone in the know
  • What is your method(s) of communication?
  • How / when will 1-on-1s and team meetings be accomplished?
Define Accountability Creates trust, deepens commitment
  • As the team leader, do you allow yourself to be held accountable?
  • How is your team accountable to one another?

Leadership Is Caught, Not Taught

Developing Leaders

Leadership is more often caught through watching qualified people than through your own individuality. Debrief each phase with three questions: What worked? What didn’t? How can we improve?

01
I do. You watch.
We talk.
The new leader shadows an experienced leader a few times, then they meet to debrief what was observed.
02
I do. You help.
We talk.
The new leader helps lead a particular area—announcements and prayer, say, while the experienced leader leads the talk. Follow up one-on-one.
03
You do. I help.
We talk.
The new leader transitions from supporting to taking leadership responsibilities. The experienced leader follows up to debrief.
04
You do. I watch.
We talk.
The new leader fulfills all the functions of leadership, with the experienced leader now looking on.
05
You do.
The new leader is successfully leading in our ministry and is placed on your team.

The Neglected Priority

Rest

Rest might be one of the most important, yet often neglected priorities in our relationship with God. Think about the last time you heard—or said: “I’m good, I’m just tired.” · “I just need a vacation soon.” · “I feel burnt out.” · “Just need more hours in the day.”

Our ability to lead people often hinges on our ability to rest well.

Jesus provides a solution to our hurried living. He modeled a life of rest amidst the demands of everyday life—in tune with his deep need to connect to the Father. Rest reveals a posture of the heart that is deeply dependent upon God. We must continually check ourselves: how much stock do I put in my production for Christ over my connectedness to Christ?
