Welcome, New Leader

Let’s get to work

We’re so glad you’re here. Stepping into student ministry matters now more than ever. This is not about showing up once a week—you’re joining a movement of what God is doing to change lives for eternity.

143,556 students are without Christ in our region. They don’t know Jesus—but change starts one student at a time—and God wants to use you.

Every game, every question, every text, every time you show up—it matters. Students need consistent voices who love them, listen, speak truth, and point them to Jesus. You may become one of the most spiritually influential people in their lives.

You’re not just a volunteer; you’re a shepherd—and you’re not doing it alone. You’re part of a team that will equip, support, and pray with you.

Will you lead with urgency, love, and courage this year? We believe God will use you in powerful ways.

GRATEFUL,
YOUR STUDENT MINISTRY TEAM

The Mission

Why We Exist

Southeast Christian Church · Mission
Connecting people to Jesus
and to one another.

That means every effort, every prayer at Southeast works to fulfill it—Student Ministry included. Our mission is biblically rooted in both:

The Great Commandment
Matthew 22:37–39
“Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind. … And a second is like it: love your neighbor as yourself.”
The Great Commission
Matthew 28:19–20
“Go therefore and make disciples of all nations … and behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age.”

As a member of the Student Ministry team—no matter your role, from leading a group to welcoming people at the door—this is what you’re signing up to do.

The Mission

Four Questions

Connecting students to Jesus and one another starts with four questions for every leader:

01
How am I connecting to Jesus?
Your connection to Christ matters more than what you offer in service to Christ. God’s Word and prayer are the fuel for an effective ministry.
02
How am I connecting to one another?
Being connected to Jesus isn’t enough. Jesus modeled community through the Trinity, but also through the disciples he walked with. Remember, ministry is a team effort.
03
How am I connecting people to Jesus?
As we are transformed, our purpose shifts. Jesus understood His mission—to seek and save the lost. He left the mission in the disciples’ hands. That mission is now yours to carry.
04
How am I connecting people to one another?
Neighborhoods, schools, workplaces and families need you to have vision for cultivating community. Belonging often opens the door of reception to Christ.

Commitments & Expectations

The Basics

01
Co-leaders + Teams
Every small group leader has at least one co-leader. You and your co-leader(s) sit on a larger team of other leaders from your grade and all leaders in your ministry (MSM or HSM).
02
On Time + Prepared
“On time” means 30 minutes before service. “Prepared” means 30 minutes spent reading the passage, writing questions, and praying for your group.
03
Year Long Commitment
August to July. Students thrive on consistency. Jesus raised the bar, not us. Your co-leader covers you for sickness or travel.
04
Model The Way
Students don’t need perfection—they need people who follow Jesus with their whole life. You model the way at church, in your neighborhood, and on social media.
05
Cultivate Community
Be a cultivator of community on your team and in the group you lead. Be the teammate you wish you had.
Your Group09
3 Wins for Student Groups

Three Wins

“High accountability, low control”
01
Connect to Jesus
Did your students open the Bible? It would be a mistake to gather with students and never open God’s living and active Word. Make this the priority.
02
Connect to one another
Did your students connect? Watch for the one sitting off to the side on their phone, and help the group pull them back in. Students just want simple relational connection.
03
One at a time
One meal, one conversation, one need, one prayer. Keep a listening ear open—listen for ways you can follow up during the week.

Leading a Group

5 Keys

01
Create an environment for discussion
Sit in a circle, on the same level. Everyone can see each other—and everyone feels free to share.
02
Turn statements into questions
Facilitate, don’t teach. Use more question marks than periods, and skip yes-or-no questions.
03
Involve every student
Don’t fill the silence with your own answer. Ask “What do you think?” and give it room—a little awkward silence never hurt anybody.
04
Don’t read off the curriculum
Know the topic ahead of time and ask in your own words. The less you look down, the more it feels like a conversation.
05
Follow up during the week
Students need someone to walk with them, not talk at them. A relationship carried through the week can change a life.
Leading Well12
When a Group Stalls

Getting Unstuck

01
Get easy wins
Blank stares usually mean the question was too hard. Ask simpler questions to build confidence, then work toward tougher ones.
02
Get fun
The first part of group is for fun, so students grow as a family. Use that time to earn the right to challenge them.
03
Get out
Experiences beyond Sunday—serving and showing up during the week—directly fuel your group time.
04
Get better questions
A great question yields as much as a great answer. Practice them before group; write a few of your own.
05
Get visual
A big sticky pad and a few markers pull everyone in. Drawing or writing answers makes the lesson stick.

MEET STUDENTS WHERE THEY ARE

WHAT STUDENTS NEED

Every student has a need. Four chairs helps us pinpoint that need. As a leader, consider the following questions and see if God might reveal how you could meet the needs of the students around you. 

Chair 1
The Lost
“Come and See”John 1:35–39
Needs

Christ-followers in their lives who are willing to enter into their world relationally.

Questions

Who has God placed right around you? Are you entering into their world? Would  people identify you as a “friend of sinners,” as Jesus was? Are you willing to leave  the comfort of your home or church to enter into the world of those without Christ?

Chair 2
The Believer
“Follow Me”John 1:43
Needs

Christ-followers giving them personalized attention to the basics of the Christian life.

Questions

Do they know God’s story? Can they articulate their own story and how it fits into  God’s story? Do they know how to confess sin, claim God’s forgiveness and walk  in the power of the Holy Spirit? Are they learning to feed themselves?

The Worker

Christ-followers calling them to action and to experience God using them.

Questions

Are they faithful, available, teachable, enthusiastic about the things of God, and responsive to leadership? Are they ministering in the Spirit? Are they living out their spiritual gifts? Are they willingly bearing their cross daily?

Disciple-Maker
“Go and Bear Fruit”John 15:15–16
Needs

To be a Christ-follower who is making disciple-making disciples.

Questions

Have they successfully discipled someone into a disciple-maker? Are they secure in their walk with the Lord? Have they committed all of their life to fulfilling Jesus’ Great Commission?

Shepherding Students16
Helping Chair 2 Grow Deep Roots

I Want to Follow Christ

Five things a new believer needs to grow before becoming a worker—ask how Jesus shaped each in His disciples.

I
Identity—who am I in Christ?
Help new believers see their new identity—otherwise, letting the old self die feels like losing something precious instead of gaining it.
W
Walk—how do I follow Christ?
“Walk as Jesus walked” (1 John 2:6). Learn His character, His priorities, and His habits.
T
Talk—what is the good news?
Grow in gospel fluency and urgency—God’s story, and how their story fits inside it.
F
Feed—how do I feed myself?
Make daily time with God a priority: prayer, worship, reading, journaling, or walking in His creation.
C
Clean—what do I do when I sin?
Walk students through daily repentance, confession, and a renewed commitment when sin creeps back in.

Baptism

Purpose: to help a student reflect on their decision for Christ and the commitment of baptism.

1.

Starting Questions

  • Tell me about your journey with Jesus so far.
  • Why do you want to get baptized?
  • Share with me your understanding of the gospel.
  • Do you have any questions or things you’re unsure about?
2.

Gospel Conversation ( see The Bridge—next page )

Genesis 3

Isaiah 59:2
Sin leads to separation from God
Romans 6:23
We deserve death, but God gifts us new life
Romans 5:8
Christ made a way back for us to God
Acts 2:38
Confession leads to forgiveness of sins

Romans 6:4

“We were therefore buried with him through baptism into death in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead through the glory of the Father, we too may live a new life.”

3.

What Is Baptism?

  • Ask the student to read Romans 6:1–7 aloud.
  • Baptism is a symbol:
Dying to our old lifeunder the water
Rising to new life in Jesuscoming up
  • Baptism doesn’t save us. It is our response to what Jesus already did.
4.

Now What?

  • Read 2 Corinthians 5:17 to 6:2.
  • Today is the day of salvation! Will you go now and be Christ’s ambassador? He wants to use you to reach others! Who will you tell?
Decision Guide20

The Bridge

1
Us + God

Genesis 1 describes the start of everything, including the start of humanity. God made us and began a relationship with us.

2

Genesis 3 describes how our relationship with God was broken by humanity’s disobedience.

We call this disobedience “sin.” God can have nothing to do with sin. Read Romans 3:23.

3

Romans 6:23 and Isaiah 59:2 describe the problem sin creates and its consequences. Sin separates us from God and causes death. There is nothing we can do to return to God on our own.

4

Romans 5:8, Romans 10:9–10 and Acts 2:38 tell us about the solution. His name is Jesus.

Jesus died for everyone’s sin, conquered death, and made a way for us to be with God again. When we trust in Jesus and choose to be baptized, we are saved!

The Toolbox

Conversations Guide

SE Students wants every leader to promote safety and awareness in tough conversations. Ministry is messy and these topics can feel intimidating—here are the guidelines for how to best proceed.

Green Light
“Green means go.”
You’ve got this. You have both the Sword and the Spirit.
  • Family problems
  • Relational problems
  • Homesickness
  • Heartfelt confessions
  • Questions about dating & sex
  • Church & theological questions
Yellow Light
“Proceed with caution.”
Mention it to a staff member for coaching.
  • Bullying
  • Pornography
  • Eating disorders
  • Drugs or alcohol
  • Anxiety or depression
  • Engagement in sexual activity
  • Gender & sexual identity questions
Red Light
“Stop and get help.”
Get a staff member and bring them into the conversation.
  • Self-injury
  • Hearing voices / hallucinations
  • Sexual & physical abuse reported
  • Suicidal / homicidal thoughts (threats to harm)
