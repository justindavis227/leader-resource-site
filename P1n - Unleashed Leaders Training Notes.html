Unleashed Leaders
Training Notes

One set of notes, run at the pace that fits your new-leader pool. This manuscript is a general plan of what to cover each session—the slides and booklet align with these notes.

01Two WeeksTwo sessions, two weeks

Session 1 and Session 2 each get their own week. A good rhythm when training pairs with an existing gathering—leaders have time between sessions for the homework and for the content to settle.

02Two HoursBoth sessions, one sitting

Run Hour 1 and Hour 2 back to back as a stand-alone training block. A good fit to kick off a school year or when a group of new leaders needs to be ready quickly—take a short break between the hours.

031 on 1 / FewSporadic interest, year-round

We never want a lengthy amount of time to pass when helping someone jump into our ministry. Use this option when new leader interest arrives sporadically throughout the year—walk them through the booklet one-on-one.

Before You Train

This training utilizes the 4 Chair Discipling framework (Dann Spader) alongside the Unleashed Leaders booklet. Room set up each week: water, pen, booklet, handouts. Pre-show: looping video (Mission · Vision · Title) and introduce the staff team present. Staff note: use the 4 Chair Inventory in your interview to help identify where someone is.

Set Up — Illustration to grab the room
  • Share name + what brought you here.
  • Booklet has a note section—no need to follow along in it.
  • IllustrationDollar store (40-60 piece) puzzle. Dump pieces on their tables. You keep the box.
  • Give them 2 minutes to complete as much as they can. Table with most completed wins a prize (your choice).
  • This training is to give you a clear picture of disciple-making. Hold the box up. If you had the puzzle picture in mind, you would know what to do.
  • Over the next 2 weeks / 2 hours we will learn how to make disciples in student ministry.
  • Another picture we have: 143,556 students. Most don’t know Jesus—but change starts one student at a time.
10
Mission + Vision — Jesus sets our mission
  • Table DiscussionWhy did Jesus come to earth? What was His mission?
  • Jesus Mission: seek & save the lost (Luke 19:10) · make & multiply disciples (Colossians 1:6).
  • Southeast’s mission: connecting people to Jesus & one another—rooted in the Great Commission + Great Commandment.
  • Southeast’s vision: unleash the full force of the church to love people one at a time. We make a large church feel small, a large mission tangible. Student ministry is your place of being unleashed.
15
Our Model — Jesus is our model
  • Table DiscussionIn your own words, define “disciple.” What are some things Jesus did to make disciples?
  • How Jesus did it: chose Twelve (Lk 6:12–16) · regularly sent (Lk 9:1–6) · engaged the crowd (Lk 9:10–17) · always followed up (Lk 9:18–20) · raised the bar (Lk 9:23–27) · invested in a few (Lk 9:28).
  • What that means for us: Small Groups—we choose twelve (ish) · Unleash—we send students out · Program—we engage the crowd · Intentionality—we follow up · Expectations—we raise the bar · One at a Time—we invest in a few.
10
Tools — what’s at our disposal?
  • Overview of what we do with STUDENTS.
  • Overview of what we do for LEADERS.
  • Point to your ministry calendar and dates where relevant.
10
Your Role — summary of your role
  • Read through the role overview and extrapolate. Then summarize: Shepherd · Harvest Worker · Teammate.
  • 1 Peter 5:2 → Shepherd: be shepherds over God’s flock—examples worth following.
  • Luke 10:2 → Harvest Worker: Luke 9:62 calls us to grip the plow. Be a worker; call others to the work.
  • 1 Corinthians 3:5–9 → Teammate: each plays a part in planting, watering, tilling. God makes it grow—use your gifts for His Kingdom.
  • “You Win When” = a student takes a next step toward Christ
Homework — 4 Chair Personal Inventory (If doing two weeks, have them bring it back to Part 2)

Week 2 / Hour 2

4 Chairs · 4 Challenges · Reach // Build // Equip // Multiply
Week 2 / Hour 2Have 4 chairs on stage to sit & move in
Room Set UpCheck-in · Water · Pen · Booklet · Your Special Touch (Set 15 minutes before people arrive)
5
Set Up + 4 Chair Overview
  • Recap as needed.
  • Chair 1 = Lost: everyone is born into this chair. “Lost” is the Biblical word used in Luke 15.
  • Chair 2 = Believer: once we place our faith in Christ to forgive our sins we move to chair 2.
  • Chair 3 = Worker: when you start fishing for people, harvesting the spiritual fields around you, you become a “worker.”
  • Chair 4 = Disciple-Maker: when you are personally reaching the lost, building up believers and equipping other workers, you have become a disciple-maker.
10
Challenge 1 — “Come and See” · John 1:39 · John 1:46 · John 4:29
  • Andrew tells Simon; Philip tells Nathanael. Invitation = come and see. Response = repent and believe.
  • Discuss 2 minWho are the lost people that naturally cross your path each day?
  • Reach = MS + HS Camp · ONE Nights · Worship Nights
  • Organically: we send students every Wed/Sun to reach their schools, homes, and communities.
10
Challenge 2 — “Follow Me” · John 1:43 · Luke 9:23 · Luke 9:57
  • Challenge = lay down your life and follow Jesus.
  • Discuss 2 minWhat does it mean to be a follower? What is expected of one?
  • Build = Groups · Weekend Worship · Retreats
  • Organically: building happens in the in-between moments—praying and reading with students as we go.
10
Challenge 3 — “Fish for Men” · Matthew 4:19 · Luke 10:2
  • Challenge = get after the work of ministry and fish for people.
  • Discuss 2 minHow do we know we’re ready to be a harvest worker? What did Jesus look for?
  • Equip = Equip Workshops · Mission Trips · Serving
  • Organically: modeling mission for our students—are we going into the fields or just telling them to?
10
Challenge 4 — “Go and Bear Fruit” · John 15:5
  • Challenge = abide in Christ and bear fruit in making disciples.
  • Discuss 2 minWhere are you strongest? Where do you need to improve to produce much fruit?
  • Chair 4 disciples aren’t just small group leaders—they use every tool, alongside the Holy Spirit, to reach, build, equip
  • Multiply = Investing in a few: not a program, raising up leaders
  • Each chair will be represented in your groups this year. Your job: help students see where they are and get to what God has next.
Homework — check with staff on any clearance needs.

Training Notes · 1 on 1 / Few

1 on 1 / Few

—
The Walk-Through
  • There is a booklet for both MSM and HSM. Use it to walk someone through a general overview of who we are as a ministry and help a potential leader get a feel for our expectations.
  • Keep it conversational—the same Mission / Model / Role / 4 Chairs content, sized for a coffee table instead of a stage.
—
4 Chair Personal Inventory
  • Ask the potential leader to fill it out before meeting—or after, sending it back to you.
  • The inventory confronts them with questions that identify where they are, and how our ministry can help them as they help others move to the next chair spiritually.
Staff Note

Use the 4 Chair Inventory in your interview to help you identify where someone is. And remember the why behind all three tracks: we never want a lengthy amount of time to pass when someone is ready to jump into our ministry.
