Welcome, New Leader

Let’s get to work

We’re so glad you’re here. Stepping into student ministry matters now more than ever. This is not about showing up once a week—you’re joining a movement of what God is doing to change lives for eternity.

143,556 students are without Christ in our region. They don’t know Jesus—but change starts one student at a time—and God wants to use you.

Every game, every question, every text, every time you show up—it matters. Students need consistent voices who love them, listen, speak truth, and point them to Jesus. You may become one of the most spiritually influential people in their lives.

You’re not just a volunteer; you’re a shepherd—and you’re not doing it alone. You’re part of a team that will equip, support, and pray with you.

Will you lead with urgency, love, and courage this year? We believe God will use you in powerful ways.

GRATEFUL,
YOUR STUDENT MINISTRY TEAM

The Mission

Why We Exist

Southeast Christian Church · Mission
Connecting people to Jesus
and to one another.

That means every effort, every prayer at Southeast works to fulfill it—Student Ministry included. Our mission is biblically rooted in both:

The Great Commandment
Matthew 22:37–39
“Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind. … And a second is like it: love your neighbor as yourself.”
The Great Commission
Matthew 28:19–20
“Go therefore and make disciples of all nations … and behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age.”

As a member of the Student Ministry team—no matter your role, from leading a group to welcoming people at the door—this is what you’re signing up to do.

Make Disciples

Four Chairs

Taking four challenges Jesus gave over the course of His ministry, we use four chairs  as a word picture for what it means to be a disciple. This creates a map of spiritual  growth into four progressive stages. Jesus modeled this process over His earthly  ministry, challenging people to move from being spiritually lost to becoming multiplying disciple-makers.

4-Chair Personal Inventory

Where Am I?

Chair 1 · Seeker—“Come & See”
Disagree = 1 · Unsure = 2 · Agree = 3
  • 1I am confident I will be with God in heaven after I die.
  • 2I could never be good enough to earn my way to heaven.
  • 3Religious acts don’t make me a Christian.
  • 4There is only one way to God—faith in His Son, Jesus.
  • 5I’ve put my faith in Christ’s death and resurrection as the only payment for my sin.
  • 6My commitment to follow Christ is a response to grace.
  • 7Those who don’t put their faith in Christ will be separated from God for eternity.
Chair 1 Total/ 21
Chair 2 · Believer—“Follow Me”
Weak = 1 · Improving = 2 · Strong = 3
  • 8I am developing the character of Christ (Gal 5:22–23).
  • 9I am developing the priorities of Christ (Matt 22:37–39).
  • 10I put Christ first in every area of my life.
  • 11I depend on God’s Spirit for strength and direction.
  • 12I spend time with God daily in His Word and prayer.
  • 13I know my spiritual gifts and use them to serve.
  • 14I’m concerned for those who don’t know Jesus.
Chair 2 Total/ 21
My next step in my disciple-making journey is…
Make Disciples21

This inventory is a simple tool for you to consider where you are and get vision for your own spiritual growth. Answer honestly—score each statement, then total each chair in the box at the bottom.

Scoring Your Inventory

Total each chair in the box at its foot. A chair score of 20–21 means you can put primary focus on the next chair while staying strong in the ones before.

Chair 3 · Worker—“Fish for People”
Weak = 1 · Improving = 2 · Strong = 3
  • 15I pursue relationships with lost people and pray for their salvation.
  • 16I know how to communicate my salvation story.
  • 17I can share the gospel simply and clearly (in two minutes).
  • 18I act on opportunities to share the gospel verbally.
  • 19I pursue discipling relationships and pray for others’ growth.
  • 20I know the basics a new believer needs to grow.
  • 21I share what God is teaching me to encourage others.
Chair 3 Total/ 21
Chair 4 · Disciple-Maker—“Bear Much Fruit”
Weak = 1 · Improving = 2 · Strong = 3
  • 22I’ve shown others how to share their faith & they’re doing it.
  • 23I regularly meet with a believer to show them how to follow Christ.
  • 24I walk with others toward becoming disciples who make disciples.
  • 25I model a disciple-making lifestyle that inspires others.
  • 26I prioritize time with lost people and disciple believers.
  • 27I’ve discipled someone who is now discipling another.
  • 28I initiate new relationships to make disciples.
Chair 4 Total/ 21

Sit With These

How do the mantras help us accomplish our mission?

01
  • How is my connection to the vine?
  • How are my students’ connections to the vine?
  • How could I encourage my team to stay connected?
02
  • Where is the Spirit moving in our church that my students could join?
  • Where is the Spirit moving in our city that we could jump in?
  • What risk would cause you to truly depend on God?
03
  • If we believed God could make a way, how could we reach more students?
  • Who is my one right now? Do my students know their one?
04
  • Where do I make excuses to avoid ministry?
  • Where might God be asking me to grip the plow?
05
  • Where can I, like Paul, spend and be spent for others (2 Cor 12:15)?
  • Where could I sacrifice more and find deeper dependence on God?
