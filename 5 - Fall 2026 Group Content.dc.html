Safety Guardrails

Student to Leader: In Person

Southeast takes seriously the responsibility of keeping our people safe.  Here are some best practices when interacting with students in person. 

Programming
•Keep students in programming. We want all students in the student room. If a student wants to talk one-on-one, wait until after program and groups end, and meet in a public area where you are visible to others.
•Appropriate interactions. Appropriate touches with a student: handshakes, high-fives, and side hugs.
•When a student's ride is late. Never leave a student waiting alone—wait with two adults. If you have a parent or guardian's permission to drive a student home, bring another cleared adult.
Counseling
•Counsel same gender. If a student of the opposite gender comes to you for counsel, connect them with a leader of the same gender.
•Be visible. Meet in a public area where you are visible to other people. Never meet in private.
•Review the Counseling Spotlight. When counseling a student, refer to the Stoplight Guide on how to best proceed in conversation.
Outside of Church
•Pair up with another leader. When planning an outside activity with students, always include another cleared adult and stay within the 1:25 student/leader ratio.
•Transporting students. Never give a student a ride home unless you have another cleared leader in the vehicle and a parent's or guardian's approval.
•Communicating digitally. When texting or using social media with students, follow the Digital Communication Safety Guardrails on the next page.

Safety Guardrails

Student to Leader: Online

When it comes to digital interactions, the following are some best practices to promote safety and create a healthy environment between leaders and students. 

Talk to Parents First
Make parents and guardians fully aware of all the digital methods that will be used to communicate with students for ministry purposes.
Keep It Plural
Want to let an absent student know they were missed, or tell someone you're proud of them? Use plural pronouns instead of singular ones. Rather than “I missed you,” say “We missed you.”
Add a Co-Leader
Adding another leader helps with accountability. Group texting is the preference for all digital communication. At no time should one-on-one video communication take place between a student and leader.
Communication Limits
Most conversations can wait until the next day—a healthy boundary. Use “scheduled sending” for messages composed outside acceptable hours (e.g. write a message at 9:30 p.m. and schedule it to go out at 7:00 a.m. the next day).
Traceable Communication
Apps with a “self-destruct mode” can create dangers when adults use them to communicate with minors. To be above reproach, only communicate with students in ways that are traceable. Never delete a conversation with a student.

HIGH CALLING

LEADER COMMITMENT

Serving with minors is a trusted leadership role. The following commitments define who we commit to be and what we commit to do so students are discipled, cared for, and protected with integrity.
What We Commit to Be
01
Christ Follower
I acknowledge Jesus Christ as Lord and will keep growing through the Bible, prayer, worship, and community in the life of the church.
02
Representative
I represent Jesus, Southeast Christian Church, Southeast Students and will support the church’s mission, beliefs, leadership, and unity.
03
Above Reproach
I will live with integrity in my words, actions, relationships, habits, and online presence, and will not knowingly misrepresent Jesus or Southeast.
04
Trustworthy
I will use my influence to protect, encourage, and disciple students—never to manipulate, isolate, shame, favor, or create inappropriate dependence.
05
Teachable
I will receive coaching, correction, and direction with humility. Teachability is part of faithful leadership.
06
Disciple-Maker
This is more than a weekly program. I will pray for students, help them feel known, and point them to Jesus and their next steps of faith.
07
Integrity
If my ability to lead with integrity becomes compromised, I will speak with Staff and, if needed, step back from serving.
08
Conflict Resolution
Following Matthew 18:15-17, I will handle conflict with humility—going directly to the person, avoiding gossip, pursuing reconciliation, and involving the right leaders when needed.
LEADER COMMITMENT{{ agreeA }}
What We Commit to Do
01
Serve Faithfully
I will serve the full ministry year—August through July—and participate in weekly gatherings, leader trainings, and all required ministry rhythms.
02
Consistent
I will be on time, prepared, and engaged when serving. Consistency builds trust with students, families, leaders, and staff.
03
Communicate
If I need to miss a gathering, event, or serving commitment, I will tell my co-leaders and Staff as early as possible.
04
Be a Teammate
I will work with co-leaders, campus staff, parents, and SE Students leadership in a way that is clear, cooperative, and aligned.
05
Safety Guidelines
I will follow all Southeast and Southeast Students safety policies and boundaries at all times, including:
Never being alone with a student in a private or isolated setting.
Appropriate physical, emotional, spiritual, relational, and digital boundaries.
Communicating with students only in transparent and approved ways.
Reporting concerns, disclosures, or unsafe situations to Staff immediately.
Agreement
By signing, I confirm I have read, understand, and agree to these commitments as a leader with Southeast Students. Failure to follow these commitments may result in being paused or removed from serving. If I have questions or cannot agree to any commitment, I will speak with Staff before serving.
